WSP: My Library of Components

1) Header

Target Audience:

The intended audience for this header are those who prefer a more modern appeal.

Client/User Needs:

The main goal this header has to provide for the client with a quick and easy way to find what they need without the hassle of having to waste their time searching it through the website. Of course, that is an option as well.

Critique:

This design stood out to me the most out of all designs I have seen. The reason this header stood out to me in particular was because it has a professional and a modern appeal to it. This header includes three slide bars, which for my personal website will include various features that the client will most likely want to browse.

2) About

Target Audience:

The target audience would be anyone interested in purchasing or selling various items, such as, clothing, electronics, etc. Anyone from every age and social group will find this website useful.

Client/User Needs:

The main goal for this website would be to allow our users to have the best and most convenient experience possible while purchasing items or merchandise.

Critique:

The design is simple and professionally laid out. The "about" section alone provides the user with many options to choose from as well as links to other sources to make the user's shopping experience more enjoyable.

3) CTA: "Call to Action"

Target Audience:

The target audience for this "call to action" in particular would be anyone interested in having the latest electronic devices. This is also intended for anyone looking for a great father's day gift!

Client/User Needs:

The main goal or purpose for this cta is to promote our merchandise to our users so in return they will most likely see this advertisement and will want to make a purchase, or at the least will consider the opportunity.

Critique:

I really love the design for this cta. What stands out to me the most is the unique pattern at the top. The pattern is an eye-catcher and will grab the user's attention right away. It also creates a "hierarchy" that will lead the user down step by step. The next eye-catcher would be the price as well as the tag in the right-hand corner. The design is crafted very well it goes nicely with the promoted item.
